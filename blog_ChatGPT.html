How smart is Chat GPT?1 May 2023

Open AI’s Chat GPT has become incredibly popular over the past months. AI and LLMs have the ability to understand natural language and generate responses that are often indistinguishable from those of a human. Students are taking advantage of this technology to help with tasks such as writing essays, creating outlines, generating summaries, and even answering questions on exams.

For instance, a student can feed a prompt into an AI writing assistant, and it will provide suggestions and even complete sentences for them. These tools can be especially helpful for students who struggle with writing or those who need to write a lot of papers in a short amount of time.

Users can use natural language to explain their coding problems to ChatGPT, which can then provide suggestions and solutions. ChatGPT can also help with debugging code by identifying errors and offering solutions. Additionally, students can use ChatGPT to learn programming concepts and improve their coding skills by asking questions and receiving explanations in a conversational manner.

“You now have a computer that can answer any question in a way that makes sense to a human”

“You now have a computer that can answer any question in a way that makes sense to a human,” said Aaron Levie, chief executive of a Silicon Valley company, Box. Chat GPT is already been banned in many institutes AP reported in Jan. Others are developing policies around the technology. “The University of Waterloo expects that students will do their own learning, and not fabricate, falsify, or otherwise pass off others’ work as their own. There are, however, ways to use many tools ethically for learning. Using ChatGPT (or similar tools that generate text, code, or visual images) for content generation and submitting it as one’s own original work is a violation of the University of Waterloo’s Student Discipline Policy”

Comparison of GPT models performance in exams

In a recent paper released on March 23rd, 2023 Open AI released the performance of their LLM models on different competitive exams. “We tested GPT-4 on a diverse set of benchmarks, including simulating exams that were originally designed for humans.4 We did no specific training for these exams.” ( source: GPT-4) GPT-4 performed significantly better on some exams compared to the previous models. For instance there was 800% improvement on the Bar exam, 223% on AP Chemistry, 220% on GRE Quantitative and 219% on USABO 2022 semifinal exam(biology olympiad).



GPT-3.5 was trained on 175 billion parameters while GPT-4 is said to be trained over a whopping 1 trillion parameters

It is worth mentioning here that GPT-3.5 was trained on 175 billion parameters while GPT-4 is said to be trained over a whopping 1 trillion parameters.
